Details on structural equation modelling

To decipher and quantify the direct and indirect pathways linking complexity to diversity across changing environments, a pSEM approach was used (Lefcheck, 2016). A set of causal path models was constructed from the hypothetical relationships identified in the literature among hydrosedimentary dynamics, habitat complexity facets of maerl and polychaete functional and taxonomic diversity (Figure 1, Annexe X for details). Each observation (a site at a given year, n = 90) was characterized in the pSEM with 1) its position of along the first axis of the hydrosedimentary stress PCA, 2) its median value for each habitat complexity metric, with nodule surface and nodule biomass as proxies of habitat amount, nodule convexity for habitat geometry and nodule trait dispersion for habitat diversity, and 3) to the total density and species richness of its polychaete assemblage as proxies of community size.

Path models

All path models were visually checked for linear model assumptions, and tested for homoscedasticity and linearity of residuals.
Total density of polychaetes, nodule surface and nodule trait dispersion were log transformed, and nodule biomass was square root transformed to linearise relationships and meet model assumptions. Convexity was also log-transformed; however, homoscedasticity and normality of residuals were not fully achieved for this variable within the piecewise structural equation model (pSEM). To assess the robustness of our results to this assumption violation, we fitted an alternative pSEM in which convexity was modelled as a response variable using a generalized linear model with a Gamma distribution and inverse link, applied to untransformed convexity values. Overall pSEM comparison based on AIC and Chi-square statistics indicated similar global fit between the Gaussian and Gamma-based pSEMs. Focusing specifically on the convexity sub-model, predicted values from both approaches did not differ significantly (mean difference not significantly different from zero) and showed comparable predictive accuracy (RMSE and MAE; see Supplementary Information). Because both approaches yielded equivalent predictions and model fit, we retained the Gaussian pSEM for the main analyses in order to facilitate comparison of standardized path coefficients across model components. Importantly, qualitative conclusions regarding the direction and relative strength of paths were consistent across both model formulations.
